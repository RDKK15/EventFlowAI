5.1 Software Should Feel Like a Business Partner
Principle

BizPart AI should feel like hiring an experienced business partner—not operating enterprise software.

Rationale

Business owners think in terms of customers, events, quotations, workers, and money—not databases, entities, and workflows.

Example

❌

Create ServiceType

✅

Let's add another service your business provides.
5.2 Natural Conversation Before Configuration
Principle

Whenever practical, BizPart should ask natural business questions instead of exposing technical configuration.

Example

Instead of:

Create RequirementDefinition

BizPart asks:

What information do you normally collect before confirming a birthday decoration booking?
5.3 Progressive Discovery
Principle

The platform should reveal capabilities gradually as the business grows.

Rationale

Small businesses should never feel overwhelmed.

Advanced features should appear when they become valuable.

5.4 Start Simple, Grow Naturally

BizPart should support this journey:

One owner

↓

Owner + spouse

↓

Small team

↓

Growing company

↓

Large business

The owner should never feel like they "outgrew" BizPart.

5.5 Every Screen Must Have a Purpose

Every screen should answer one question:

"What business problem does this solve?"

If no clear answer exists...

The screen probably shouldn't exist.

5.6 Every Click Should Save Time

BizPart exists to reduce business effort.

Every feature should either:

Save time
Reduce mistakes
Increase understanding
Increase revenue
Improve customer experience

Otherwise its value should be questioned.

5.7 Technology Should Be Invisible

Users shouldn't think:

"I'm configuring software."

They should think:

"I'm explaining my business."

The technology should disappear behind the experience.

5.8 Learning Never Stops

BizPart should continuously improve by observing:

Business configuration
Customer interactions
Events
Quotations
Inventory
Corrections
Feedback

Learning is continuous.

5.9 Customization Without Complexity

Businesses should feel:

"This understands my business."

without requiring hundreds of settings.

Customization comes from understanding—not excessive configuration.

5.10 Business First, Features Second

Features should never exist because competitors have them.

Every feature must solve a validated business problem.

No feature-first development.

5.11 The Platform Should Age Gracefully

Adding new modules should make BizPart feel more complete—not more complicated.

The product should become more capable while remaining approachable.

5.12 Trust Is More Important Than Intelligence

The smartest AI is useless if owners don't trust it.

BizPart should always choose:

Predictability
Transparency
Consistency

over flashy behavior.

5.13 Build for the Business Owner, Not the Software Engineer

Every major product decision should ask:

"Would a busy business owner understand this without training?"

If the answer is "no," we should redesign the experience before asking the user to learn more.

🚫 Constitutional Violations
Building software that feels like enterprise software instead of a trusted business partner.
Exposing technical implementation details (such as database entities, internal IDs, or configuration terminology) directly to business owners.
Forcing long, mandatory setup or onboarding processes before users can experience value.
Requiring technical knowledge to operate the platform.
Designing features around technology instead of validated business problems.
Introducing complexity when a simpler solution achieves the same outcome.
Adding features solely because competitors have them.
Prioritizing AI capabilities over usability, trust, or owner control.
Making the product harder to use as new features are added.
Asking users to adapt to the software instead of adapting the software to the business.
Asking business owners to think like software administrators instead of business owners.

Examples include exposing concepts such as:

RequirementDefinition
ServiceType
ValueType
CollectionStage
Database configuration terminology

Instead, BizPart AI should communicate using the natural language of the business.

🔒 Constitutional Principle

BizPart AI should feel less like software and more like an experienced business partner that understands how the owner's business works, grows with the business, and quietly removes complexity instead of adding it.
